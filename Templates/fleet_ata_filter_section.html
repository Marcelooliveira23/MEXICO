Chart Parameters Explained

What it shows: Breakdown of your fleet into operational status categories.

  • Operational — Aircraft with no open issues, fully available for operations
  • Troubleshooting Open — Aircraft with open troubleshooting items under diagnosis
  • AOG/Out of Service — Aircraft grounded due to critical failures
  • MEL Open — Aircraft with deferred maintenance items approved for operation

Why it matters: Helps identify fleet availability and dispatch capability at a glance.

What it shows: For each aircraft, displays three key metrics:

  • Bar height = Open issue weight (failures, MEL, troubleshooting items severity combined)
  • Health Index (number inside) = Operational health score (0-100) calculated from issues, age, utilization
  • Flight Hours (FH) = Cumulative hours flown, indicates utilization and wear level

Color coding:

  • Green — Excellent health, low risk
  • Yellow — Fair health, moderate maintenance load
  • Red — Poor health, high priority maintenance needed

Why it matters: Prioritizes which aircraft need immediate maintenance before next operation.

What it shows: Frequency of failures by ATA (aircraft system) chapter over time.

ATA Codes Reference:

  • ATA 21 — Air Conditioning & Pressurization
  • ATA 22 — AutoFlight (autopilot)
  • ATA 23 — Communications
  • ATA 24 — Electrical Power
  • ATA 26 — Fire Protection
  • ATA 27 — Flight Controls
  • ATA 29 — Hydraulic Power
  • ATA 32 — Landing Gear
  • ATA 34 — Navigation
  • ATA 49 — Auxiliary Power Unit (APU)
  • ATA 71 — Power Plant (Engines)
  • ATA 73 — Engine Fuel & Control
  • ATA 79 — Starting Systems

Why it matters: Identifies systemic issues across the fleet; helps with preventive maintenance planning.

How Health Index is Calculated:

Health Index = 100 - (failures × 2) - (open items × 5) - (MEL items × 4) - (AOG items × 25)

Component Weights:

  • Each historic failure = -2 points
  • Each open troubleshooting item = -5 points
  • Each open MEL item = -4 points
  • Each AOG/grounded instance = -25 points (highest penalty)

Range: Minimum 10, Maximum 100

  • 90-100 — EXCELLENT
  • 70-89 — GOOD
  • 50-69 — FAIR
  • <50 — POOR

Purpose: Single number to quickly assess each aircraft's operational readiness.
